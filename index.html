When do we use Past Simple?

  • For completed actions in the past
  • For past events at a specific time
  • For sequences of past actions
  • For past habits (used to + verb)

Regular Verbs (+ ed)

Regular verbs add -ed to form the past simple:

Base Form Past Simple Business Example
work worked I worked late yesterday.
negotiate negotiated We negotiated the contract.
close closed They closed the deal.
sign signed She signed the agreement.
Spelling Rules:
  • Most verbs: add -ed ‚Üí worked, called, started
  • Verbs ending in -e: add -d ‚Üí liked, arrived, agreed
  • Verbs ending in consonant + y: y ‚Üí ied ‚Üí study/studied, carry/carried
  • Short verbs (CVC): double consonant + ed ‚Üí stop/stopped, plan/planned

Negative Form

Subject + did not (didn't) + base verb

Subject did not / didn't Base Verb
I / You / He / She / We / They did not / didn't work / go / buy

Examples:

  • I didn't work yesterday.
  • She didn't attend the conference.
  • We didn't sign the agreement.
  • They didn't go to the meeting.

Question Form

Did + subject + base verb?

Did Subject Base Verb
Did you / he / she / they work / go / buy?

Examples:

  • Did you close the deal? - Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.
  • Did he attend the meeting? - Yes, he did. / No, he didn't.
  • Did they buy the equipment? - Yes, they did. / No, they didn't.
